What even is calculus?

We use calculus to find a rate of change in a number or the area undeneath of a curve. But to start with calculus we need to understand the most basic of graphs, such as the parabolic, regression and cubic.


We'll start with the easiest, the regression. This is also known as a linear line and follows the following formula: $$ y = mx + c $$ \(y\) being the y co-ordinate
\(m\) being the gradient or slope of the line
\(x\) being the x co-ordinate
\(c\) being the y-intercept of the line



The form of this line is a straight line that is either positive (increasing) or negative (decreasing), and the slope of a line is controled with the gradient or \(m\)

Now there is the parabolic curve, which is indicated when the equation contains a variable to the power of 2 (for e.g. \(x^2\)). A parabolic equation looks like this: $$ y = x^2 $$


But because this is a curve, the slope of the curve changes and isn't a constant like a straight regression line. So to find the gradient equation we have to find what is called a 'derivative'


Derivatives

To find a derivative we use this simple formula: $$ \frac{dy}{dx} x^n = nx^{n-1} $$ This equation is saying "The derivative of \(x^2\) is \(nx\) to the power of \(n-1\)" (n being the power that is applied to the x, which in the case of a parabola is 2)


With this, now we can find the gradient equation of a parabola like so: $$ \frac{dy}{dx} x^2 = 2x^{2-1} = 2x $$ Now we know that the gradient for a parabola at the point \(y\) is \(2x\)
